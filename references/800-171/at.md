The Awareness and Training family provides security requirements to ensure that managers and users of organizational systems are aware of security risks and are trained in their responsibilities for protecting CUI.

**Source:** Derived from SP 800-53 Rev 5 Awareness and Training (AT) family.

---

## 03.02.01 Literacy Training and Awareness

Provide security awareness training to system users (including managers, senior executives, and contractors) focused on recognizing and reporting potential indicators of insider threat, social engineering, and other CUI-related threats.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** AT-2, AT-2(2), AT-2(3), AT-2(5)

**Discussion:** Organizations provide literacy training and awareness on recognizing and reporting potential indicators of insider threats, social engineering attacks, and advanced persistent threats. Training is provided upon initial hire and periodically thereafter.

---

## 03.02.02 Role-Based Training

Provide role-based security training to personnel with assigned security roles and responsibilities before authorizing access or performing assigned duties, when required by system changes, and periodically thereafter.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** AT-3, AT-3(5)

**Discussion:** Role-based training addresses the specific responsibilities of individuals with security roles including system administrators, network administrators, security officers, and personnel handling CUI. Training includes processing of personally identifiable information.

---

## 03.02.03 Training Records

Document and monitor individual security training activities. Retain training records for an organization-defined time period.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** AT-4

**Discussion:** Organizations document and monitor individual security training activities including basic awareness training and role-based training. Training records include dates of training, type of training, and training attendance.
