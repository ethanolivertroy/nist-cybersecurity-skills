The Personnel Security family provides security requirements for screening individuals prior to authorizing access to systems containing CUI and ensuring that systems are protected during and after personnel actions such as terminations and transfers.

**Source:** Derived from SP 800-53 Rev 5 Personnel Security (PS) family.

---

## 03.09.01 Personnel Screening

Screen individuals prior to authorizing access to systems containing CUI. Rescreen individuals in accordance with organization-defined conditions.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** PS-3

**Discussion:** Personnel screening activities include background checks, credit checks, and citizenship verification as appropriate for the information being accessed. Organizations define the screening criteria and conditions for rescreening. The level of screening is commensurate with the sensitivity of CUI.

---

## 03.09.02 Personnel Termination and Transfer

When individuals are terminated or transferred, protect CUI by terminating system access and retrieving all CUI-related organizational system-related property in a timely manner. Conduct exit interviews. Revoke authentication credentials. Retain access to organizational information and systems formerly controlled by the terminated individual.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** PS-4, PS-5

**Discussion:** Organizations disable access promptly upon termination and review access authorizations upon transfer. System-related property includes hardware authentication tokens, system administration/security manuals, keys, identification cards, and building passes. Exit interviews include reminders about CUI handling obligations.

---

## 03.09.03 External Personnel Security

Establish personnel security requirements, including security roles and responsibilities, for external providers. Require external providers to comply with personnel security policies and procedures. Monitor provider compliance.

**Mapped SP 800-53 Controls:** PS-7

**Discussion:** External personnel include contractors, subcontractors, vendors, and other external parties who have access to systems containing CUI. Organizations establish and enforce personnel security requirements for external providers through contracts, agreements, and monitoring.
