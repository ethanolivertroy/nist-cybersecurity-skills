# NIST SP 800-53A Rev 5 — PM: Program Management Assessment Procedures

Assessment procedures for the Program Management family evaluate whether the organization establishes and maintains an organization-wide information security and privacy program.

---

## PM-1: Information Security Program Plan

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization develops, disseminates, and reviews/updates an organization-wide information security program plan.

**Examine:** Information security program plan; risk management strategy; system security plans across the organization.

**Interview:** Senior agency information security officer; organizational personnel responsible for information security program planning.

**Test:** Program plan distribution mechanisms; plan review and update processes.

**Determination Statements:**
- PM-01a. — An organization-wide information security program plan is developed and disseminated that provides an overview of requirements for the security program and a description of the management controls and common controls in place or planned
- PM-01b. — The information security program plan is reviewed at an organization-defined frequency
- PM-01c. — The plan is updated to address organizational changes and problems identified during plan implementation or control assessments

---

## PM-2: Information Security Program Leadership Role

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization appoints a senior agency information security officer with the mission and resources to manage the security program.

**Examine:** Information security program plan; organizational charts; role descriptions for senior information security officer.

**Interview:** Senior agency information security officer; organizational leadership.

**Test:** N/A (primarily examine and interview).

**Determination Statements:**
- PM-02 — A senior agency information security officer is appointed with the mission and resources to coordinate, develop, implement, and maintain an organization-wide information security program

---

## PM-9: Risk Management Strategy

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization develops a comprehensive strategy to manage risk to organizational operations and assets, individuals, and other organizations.

**Examine:** Risk management strategy; organizational risk assessments; system security plans; risk tolerance documentation.

**Interview:** Organizational personnel responsible for risk management; senior leadership.

**Test:** Risk management processes and workflows.

**Determination Statements:**
- PM-09a. — A comprehensive risk management strategy for the organization is developed that addresses security and privacy risk
- PM-09b. — The risk management strategy is implemented consistently across the organization
- PM-09c. — The risk management strategy is reviewed and updated at an organization-defined frequency or as required

---

## PM-10: Authorization Process

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization manages the authorization process for systems.

**Examine:** Authorization process documentation; system authorization records; risk acceptance documentation.

**Interview:** Authorizing officials; information system security officers; organizational personnel responsible for authorization process.

**Test:** Authorization process workflows.

**Determination Statements:**
- PM-10a. — A security and privacy authorization process is managed for organizational systems
- PM-10b. — An organization-defined frequency for reauthorization is established

---

## PM-11: Mission and Business Process Definition

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization defines mission and business processes with consideration for information security and privacy.

**Examine:** Mission/business process documentation; risk management strategy; system security plans.

**Interview:** Mission/business owners; organizational personnel responsible for risk management.

**Test:** Processes for incorporating security into mission/business process definition.

**Determination Statements:**
- PM-11a. — Mission and business processes are defined with consideration for information security and the resulting risk to organizational operations, assets, individuals, and other organizations
- PM-11b. — Information protection needs arising from the defined mission and business processes are determined and included in security plans
