# NIST SP 800-53A Rev 5 — PT: PII Processing and Transparency Assessment Procedures

Assessment procedures for the PII Processing and Transparency family evaluate whether the organization manages PII processing authority, purposes, consent, and transparency.

---

## PT-1: Policy and Procedures

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization develops, documents, disseminates, reviews, and updates PII processing and transparency policy and procedures.

**Examine:** PII processing and transparency policy; PII processing procedures; privacy plan; system security plan.

**Interview:** Organizational personnel responsible for privacy policy; privacy officer.

**Test:** Organizational processes for managing PII processing policy.

**Determination Statements:**
- PT-01a. — A PII processing and transparency policy is developed, documented, and disseminated
- PT-01b. — PII processing and transparency procedures are developed, documented, and disseminated
- PT-01c. — The policy and procedures are reviewed and updated at an organization-defined frequency

---

## PT-2: Authority to Process Personally Identifiable Information

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization determines and documents the legal authority for processing PII.

**Examine:** PII processing and transparency policy; privacy impact assessments; legal authorities documentation; system of records notices; privacy act statements.

**Interview:** Privacy officer; legal counsel; organizational personnel responsible for PII processing.

**Test:** PII processing authority documentation and review processes.

**Determination Statements:**
- PT-02a. — The legal authority that permits the processing of personally identifiable information is determined and documented
- PT-02b. — Processing is limited to only that which is authorized

---

## PT-3: Personally Identifiable Information Processing Purposes

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization identifies and documents the purposes for processing PII.

**Examine:** PII processing and transparency policy; purpose specification documentation; privacy impact assessments; system security plan.

**Interview:** Privacy officer; organizational personnel responsible for PII processing.

**Test:** PII processing purpose documentation processes.

**Determination Statements:**
- PT-03a. — The purpose(s) for processing personally identifiable information are identified and documented
- PT-03b. — The processing of PII is limited to only those purposes that are identified

---

## PT-4: Consent

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization implements mechanisms to obtain consent for PII processing.

**Examine:** PII processing and transparency policy; consent forms and mechanisms; privacy notices; system configuration settings.

**Interview:** Privacy officer; organizational personnel responsible for consent management; system developers.

**Test:** Consent collection mechanisms; consent recording and tracking systems.

**Determination Statements:**
- PT-04a. — Mechanisms are implemented to obtain consent from individuals for the processing of their PII
- PT-04b. — Consent is obtained prior to PII collection when required

---

## PT-5: Privacy Notice

**Assessment Objective:** Determine if the organization provides effective notice to individuals regarding PII processing.

**Examine:** PII processing and transparency policy; privacy notices; privacy act statements; website privacy policies; system security plan.

**Interview:** Privacy officer; organizational personnel responsible for privacy notices.

**Test:** Privacy notice delivery mechanisms; notice accessibility verification.

**Determination Statements:**
- PT-05a. — Effective notice is provided to individuals regarding the processing of their PII
- PT-05b. — Privacy notices describe the purpose of PII collection, use and sharing practices, and individual rights
